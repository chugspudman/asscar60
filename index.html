Rules and Information

Intro

The world of ASSCAR we all know and love, but like fantasy football. I imagine the other ASSCAR league is still going on in the world, and ASSCAR60 is another form of the sport altogether. Like NASCAR and F1.

Overall Structure

Each season is 1 month long of real-time. There are 20 races in a season, one per weekday, and they occur at 8PM eastern. Qualifiers take place 10 minutes before the race. Each race is 60 laps.

Each manager controls 1 team, with a roster between 8 and 10 drivers. Each team fields two cars per race. The races are relays, and each car must have 3 drivers across the course of the race. Drivers cannot switch cars during a race, but their car assignment and relay position can be changed outside of the race.

The driver who begins the race is the Opener, the one who finishes the race is the Closer, and the Bridge is between them. The minimum number of laps allowed for a driver's stint is 5, and the maximum is 40, meaning different drivers can be racing for different lengths of time as determined by the manager.

Managers will manage their team by drafting drivers, setting relay plans, completing driver trades with other managers, and selecting weekly driver trainings and car upgrades.

Drafts

There is an opening draft before each season, followed by a vote for the Initiation Martyr for that season. Then, between races 10 and 11 there is a rookie draft.

Opening Draft

For every season except the first, a team's roster will be entirely cleared and all drivers will once again be eligible for draft. Then, new drivers will be generated and added to the pool for a total of 60 drivers. Free agents from previous seasons are relegated to lower leagues.

The draft proceeds in reverse-standings snake order, and the managers will each pick drivers to sign over the course of 8 rounds, for a total of 48 signed drivers by the end. The remaining 12 drivers will then be eligible candidates for the Initiation Martyr of that season. Whoever is picked is martyred, and the remaining 11 drivers become Free Agents.

Rookie Draft

Between races 10 and 11 there is a rookie draft. 24 rookie drivers are generated, and generally have worse stats but higher potential than opening draft drivers.

The draft proceeds in reverse-standings snake order for two rounds, for a total of 12 newly signed rookie drivers by the end. The remaining 12 rookies become free agents. Then, each team must choose a driver to release from their roster to free agency, for a final total of 9 drivers on each team.

Qualifiers

10 minutes before each race, qualifiers are run. Each car for each team runs 3 laps during the qualifiers. The first lap is done by the car's assigned Opener, the second lap by the assigned Bridge, and the third lap by the assigned Closer.

The fastest car will be put in pole position and have no time penalty assigned to their first lap. The second fastest car will be put in the 2nd position and will have a penalty of 0.2 seconds added to their first lap. Third will have a penalty of 0.4, and so on to 12th place with a penalty of 2.2 seconds.

These time penalties are only added to the cars' first laps. The results of the qualifiers are posted in the Race Feed and the Running Order is updated accordingly before the race begins.

Stats

Driver Stats

Pace
The driver's raw lap-speed ability. Higher Pace lowers normal lap time, especially on the overall course length portion.
Control
The driver's ability to handle turns and chicanes cleanly. Higher Control reduces mishap risk and softens the time cost of technical course sections.
Overtaking
The driver's passing and defending skill. Higher Overtaking helps a driver win overtake attempts and also deny attempts from other drivers.
Stamina
How well the driver maintains performance over a stint. Higher Stamina slows the degradation of Control and Overtaking as the driver stays in the car.
Technical
How well the driver manages the car. Higher Technical helps reduce the car's Speed and Handling degradation during that driver's stint.
Weird
The driver's connection to strange race events. Higher Weird increases the chance of miraculous/anomalous effects happening around them.
Potential
How much additional room the driver has for growth. Will decrease as they undergo additional training.

Car Stats

Speed
The car's raw straight-line performance. Higher Speed lowers baseline lap time alongside driver Pace.
Handling
The car's ability to survive turns and chicanes cleanly. Higher Handling reduces mishap risk and helps with technical course-section time costs.
Durability
How well the car holds together over the race. Higher Durability slows the degradation of Speed and Handling across laps.
Feedback
How well the car communicates with the driver. Higher Feedback helps reduce the driver's Control and Overtaking degradation during a stint.
Weird
The car's own strange-event affinity. It stacks with driver Weird to increase the chance of anomalous effects.

How They Interact

  • Driver Pace and car Speed combine to make the basic lap faster.
  • Driver Control and car Handling combine to handle turns and chicanes, lowering mishap risk and reducing time lost through difficult sections.
  • Driver Overtaking determines both attacking and defending during pass attempts.
  • Driver Stamina protects driver skills from fading during a stint, while car Feedback helps with that protection.
  • Car Durability protects car stats from fading during the race, while driver Technical helps with that protection.
  • Driver Weird plus car Weird together determine how likely strange effects are to happen.

Trainings and Upgrades

Each week, every manager will be given the same randomized set of 3 choices of trainings and upgrades to choose from. Trainings are for drivers, and upgrades are for cars. Car stats do not have a cap, but each driver stat does. These caps are hidden, and can only be discovered by training.

Every time a driver undergoes training, their Potential decreases by 1 unless a cap was discovered. When a stat cap is discovered it is publicly recorded on that driver's racer card with a small triangle in the top left corner of that stat tile.

The only drivers eligible for a given week's training are the ones who participated in at least 1 race that week. If a training or upgrade is not used during the week, it does not carry over to the following week for later use.

While stats can temporarily degrade during a race for both drivers and cars, those degradations completely resolve between races and are not permanent.

Courses

There are 4 courses in the league: Race City, New Torque City, Acceleton, and Suzuka. Each has length, straights, turns, chicanes, and chances for the types of weather conditions.

Length determines the base lap time. Straights are where overtakes are allowed to occur. Turns and chicanes are each given a severity rating from 1-5, with likelihood for mishaps increasing respectively.

Weather Conditions

Sunny
No added mishap risk. This is the baseline condition.
Rain
Slightly increases mishap chance in turns and chicanes. Drivers/cars with better Control and Handling resist this better.
Snow
Moderately increases mishap chance in turns and chicanes. Again, Control and Handling help offset this.
Churn
An arcane, esoteric roiling of reality. Does not increase the general chance of mishaps or strange events. Instead, when strange effects do happen, Churn allows special, more severe Churning-only effects to occur.

Championship and MVD

Team Championship

Each car is awarded points based on the position it finishes a race in. A team scores points for both of its cars. At the end of the 20th race, all teams' points are tallied and the team with the highest number is declared the champion of that season. The opening draft for the following season is done in reverse-standings order.

1st: 252nd: 183rd: 154th: 12 5th: 106th: 87th: 68th: 4 9th: 210th: 111th: 012th: 0 DNF: 0

Most Valuable Driver

Each driver is assigned a proportional share of their cars' points at the end of each race based on how long they drove during it. The Opener is then given an extra 0.01 points, and the Closer is given an extra 0.02 points.

At the end of the 20th race, the league's Most Valuable Driver (MVD) is calculated by tallying each driver's points from across the season. This can sometimes result in a tie. There is no mechanical advantage to winning this.
